Supreme Court hears cases on transgender athletes

  • Image Rights: Kevin Dietsch/Getty Images

The Supreme Court heard two cases Tuesday about whether states can bar transgender athletes from participating in sports that align with their gender rather than their assigned sex as birth.

Transgender rights activists arguing in favor of integration argue that the disputed state bans not only violate federal anti-bias statutes but also directly contradict the U.S. Constitution's guarantee of equal protection under the Fourteenth Amendment.

Meanwhile, opponents of integration argue that allowing transgender athletes to participate in sports that align with their gender would give them unfair advantages, impacting cisgender people's opportunities to succeed.
